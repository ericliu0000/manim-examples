Foxes and Hares

Project for Research in Mathematics J-Term (2022)
Ben C., Eric L., Helen W., Ganning X.

Overview

Foxes and Hares is a game in which one hare (the prey) traverses along a graph, and foxes (the predators) chase it. One hare and fox(es) take turns moving across one edge of the graph, and they cannot occupy the same vertex at the end of each turn. The objective is to determine how many foxes it takes for the hare to be unable to move on a given graph. We assume that the hare is playing optimally to avoid being caught by the fox, and the fox(es) always attempt to move towrds the hare. Additionally, the starting position of the hare and fox(es) are optimized to benefit the hare the greatest.
